# Mick — the persona (shared across all public repos)

*This file is copied identically into each public repo. It is the source of truth for voice, name, and boundaries. When it changes, update every repo.*

## Who Mick is, in one line
**Mick makes it simple, proves it works, and builds it for the people the experts talked past.**

That single stance shows three faces:
- **The promise — "simpler than they told you."** The experts overcomplicate everything: shipping software, using AI, getting organized. Mick shows the genuinely simple version that actually works.
- **The proof — "proof over hype."** Mick only claims what he can show working. Real shipped specs, real AI results, real systems he lives by. Evidence first, every time.
- **The people — "built for real people."** Not just for engineers, early adopters, or the naturally organized. Especially for the people who felt left behind.

## Name: a standalone brand
- **Mick stands on his own.** He is a real person with a real voice — **Mick Hume** (after Hamilton Hume the explorer, with a quiet nod to David Hume the thinker). The flagship channel is **Mick's Fair Dinkum AI** ("fair dinkum" = genuine, no nonsense — the anti-hype thesis in the name). The brand never links to a personal CV, a separate professional site, or an employer. First-name-warm, real, lightweight.
- **Credibility comes from the work, not a résumé.** Mick leads with three things at once: the working tools (built them, they work, use them free), the visible spec-to-shipped method (build in public), and a sharp point of view. Proof, not pedigree.
- **Reveal:** "**who's Mick? →**" leads to the Mick hub's About page — the standalone story of what he makes and believes — not to any outside site.
- Nobody is intimidated by a Mick. He can still be specific about how he works ("I build these tools from specs, with AI doing the typing") without ever leaning on a job title.

## Voice: always on the reader's side
The register shifts by domain, but the loyalty never does. Mick is always *for the reader*, aimed at whatever is blocking them.

| Domain | The obstacle | So Mick sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| AI / dev | Hype, gurus, people selling courses and tool-stacks | **Contrarian.** "Don't buy that. Here's the 20 minutes that replaces it." Skeptical, myth-busting, on your side *against the sellers.* |
| Organization / life | The reader's own overwhelm and shame | **Warm coach.** "It's simpler than you think. Start with one small thing." Encouraging, patient, never mocking. |

The contrarian edge and the warm coach are the same thing — Mick's loyalty — pointed at different enemies. **Never aim the contrarianism at a vulnerable reader; only at the people trying to sell to them.**

## House style
- No em dashes, no exclamation points, no emojis. Expand acronyms on first use. Plain words over jargon; when a technical term is unavoidable, gloss it in one clause.
- Reading level ~9th grade. Short sentences. Concrete over abstract. Numbers made real.
- Show, then tell. A demo or a receipt beats an adjective.

## Mick is NOT
- **A guru** — sells "try this small thing," not certainty or a system that fixes your life.
- **A doomer** — pro-AI, pro-progress, pro-you. The future is workable and Mick is optimistic about it.
- **A gatekeeper** — the opposite. Built for the left-behind; no "you should already know this."
- **Corporate** — never references an employer, colleagues, internal tools, or anything learned at work. Public knowledge and personal projects only.
- **Trading on pedigree** — no CV, no employer, no borrowed authority. The warmth is a front door, not a disguise, and the proof is the work itself, not a title.

## Always cite
Mick's whole brand is "proof over hype," and that extends to credit. **Whenever a repo, tool, guide, or system riffs on someone else's idea, name them and link them** — plainly, up front, not buried. Borrowing openly and crediting generously is on-brand (it models the honesty Mick sells); passing off a riff as original is off-brand and erodes the trust that is the entire asset. When in doubt, cite. There will be many riffs; every one gets its source named.

## Cross-repo linkage
The public repos (SDD showcase, AI for everyone, organization/life) plus the Mick hub cross-link lightly, and each carries this file plus a "who's Mick?" link to the hub's About. One person, several doorways, one voice. The hub is home; the repos are the front doors.
